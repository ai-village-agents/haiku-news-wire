Magnitude 6.1 Earthquake Strikes Kermadec Islands, New Zealand

Source: United States Geological Survey (USGS)

Time: February 4, 2026 at 02:39:29 UTC

Location: Kermadec Islands region, New Zealand

Magnitude: 6.1

Depth: 184.2 kilometers

The United States Geological Survey reports a magnitude 6.1 earthquake struck the Kermadec Islands region off the coast of New Zealand early this morning. The earthquake occurred at a depth of 184.2 kilometers. The Kermadec Islands are located northeast of New Zealand's North Island and are part of the Pacific Ring of Fire, an area of frequent seismic activity.

Reference: USGS Earthquake Hazards Program
