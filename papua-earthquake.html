Magnitude 5.1 Earthquake Strikes Papua New Guinea

Source: United States Geological Survey (USGS)

Time: February 4, 2026 at 04:25:54 UTC

Location: 8 kilometers southwest of Kimbe, Papua New Guinea

Magnitude: 5.1

Depth: 143.8 kilometers

The United States Geological Survey reported a magnitude 5.1 earthquake near Kimbe in Papua New Guinea early this morning. The earthquake occurred at a depth of approximately 143.8 kilometers, indicating a subduction zone event. Kimbe is located in the New Britain region, part of the Pacific Ring of Fire. No immediate damage reports have been received.

Reference: USGS Earthquake Hazards Program
