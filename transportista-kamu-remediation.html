United States and Mexico Announce Course of Remediation at Transportista KAMU (Formerly Liber Gennesys)

Joint action under the USMCA Rapid Response Labor Mechanism ensures cross-border drivers in Tijuana can exercise their rights.

Byline: USTR Press Office Timestamp: Feb 3, 2026 • 1:05 PM PT (discovery) Source: USTR.gov
Tijuana, Baja California, Mexico — Cross-Border Transportation Services
11th USMCA Rapid Response Labor Mechanism Case

The United States and Mexico today announced a course of remediation at Transportista KAMU, formerly Liber Gennesys, following a petition under the United States-Mexico-Canada Agreement’s Rapid Response Labor Mechanism (RRM). The actions secure reinstatement and restitution for three workers and strengthen protections for freedom of association at the cross-border transportation services company in Tijuana, Baja California.

This is the eleventh—and most recent—RRM remediation to date, underscoring continued binational commitment to upholding worker rights in critical supply chain corridors. The case centers on allegations that drivers and logistics staff supporting U.S.-Mexico freight operations faced retaliation after seeking to organize with the Sindicato de Transportistas de la Cadenas de Suministro (SITRABICS).

Key Outcomes
  • Reinstatement of three workers with full backpay and benefits; where reinstatement is not feasible, payment of severance at an agreed level.
  • Development of a facility access protocol for SITRABICS to ensure the petitioning union can meet freely with members and eligible workers.
  • Expungement of disciplinary records for workers who experienced retaliation or interference with their labor rights.
  • Implementation—and facility-wide training—on a neutrality statement with a zero-tolerance policy for anti-union discrimination.
  • Structured worker briefings to inform all personnel about the findings and the right to organize without fear of reprisal.

“Today’s remediation delivers concrete relief for impacted drivers and sets clear expectations for neutrality and access going forward,” said U.S. officials, noting the agreement’s focus on transparency and enforceability.

Remediation Details

Under the agreement, Transportista KAMU will reinstate the three identified workers with full backpay and benefits accrued during their separation. Should any worker elect not to return, the company must provide severance under terms acceptable to the parties. The company will codify a facility access protocol allowing SITRABICS to speak with workers on-site without interference, ensuring that cross-border drivers—who serve U.S. and Mexican supply chains—can receive information about collective representation.

To address past retaliation, Transportista KAMU will expunge disciplinary entries tied to protected activity and issue a neutrality statement that commits management to zero-tolerance for intimidation, discrimination, or obstruction of organizing efforts. The company will deliver comprehensive trainings to supervisors and staff to embed the policy, and host worker briefings that explain the denial-of-rights findings, the remediation steps, and channels for reporting concerns.

U.S. and Mexican labor authorities will monitor implementation to ensure timely delivery of benefits and sustained compliance. The remediation reinforces that the RRM remains an effective tool to safeguard freedom of association and collective bargaining in workplaces that underpin bilateral trade.
