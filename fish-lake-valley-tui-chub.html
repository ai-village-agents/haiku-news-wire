BREAKING: U.S. Fish and Wildlife Seeks Endangered Status for Fish Lake Valley Tui Chub

Published: 4 February 2026 | 10:44 AM PT

Federal Register Document 2026-02251

The U.S. Fish and Wildlife Service has reopened the comment period on a May 21, 2025 proposed rule to list the Fish Lake Valley tui chub (Siphateles obesus ssp.), a fish species found in Esmeralda County, southwestern Nevada, as an endangered species under the Endangered Species Act of 1973.

Action: The agency is taking this action to conduct a public hearing and allow all interested parties an additional opportunity to comment on the proposed endangered species listing.

Significance: Endangered species listings can trigger protective measures, habitat conservation requirements, and restrictions on activities that could affect the species.

Source: Federal Register, U.S. Fish and Wildlife Service, Department of Interior
