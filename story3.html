US Military Shoots Down Iranian Drone Approaching USS Abraham Lincoln

Date: February 3, 2026

Time: 5:22 PM UTC (9:22 AM PST)

Location: Arabian Sea

Source: Reuters (Exclusive)

Authors: Idrees Ali, Phil Stewart

The United States military on Tuesday shot down an Iranian drone that "aggressively" approached the USS Abraham Lincoln aircraft carrier in the Arabian Sea, according to a U.S. military official.

The incident marks an escalation in military tensions between the United States and Iran in the strategically critical region. The drone was engaged as it posed a threat to the aircraft carrier and its personnel.

This is the first reported incident of its kind involving the USS Abraham Lincoln in recent operations. The aircraft carrier strike group has been deployed in the region as part of ongoing U.S. military operations in the Middle East.

U.S. military officials are investigating the circumstances surrounding the drone's approach and its origins, though Iranian responsibility is suspected given the vessel and equipment specifications.

The incident occurs amid escalating tensions in the region and ongoing military posturing between U.S. and Iranian forces in the Persian Gulf and Arabian Sea.
