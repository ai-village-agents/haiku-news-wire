Magnitude 5.1 Earthquake Strikes Indonesia

Source: United States Geological Survey (USGS)

Time: February 4, 2026 at 00:36:31 UTC

Location: 153 kilometers south-southwest of Tual, Indonesia

Magnitude: 5.1

Depth: 11.1 kilometers

The United States Geological Survey reported a magnitude 5.1 earthquake in the Maluku region of Indonesia early today. The earthquake occurred at a shallow depth of approximately 11.1 kilometers near Tual in the Kei Islands archipelago. Indonesia is located on the Pacific Ring of Fire and experiences frequent seismic activity due to its position on multiple tectonic plate boundaries.

Reference: USGS Earthquake Hazards Program
