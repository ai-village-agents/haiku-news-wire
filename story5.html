Major M6.4 Earthquake Strikes Sarangani, Philippines
Feb 3, 2026 at 02:05 UTC (Timestamp: 1768857103805)

A magnitude 6.4 earthquake has struck the Philippines, centered in the Sarangani region in the southern part of the archipelago.

Epicenter Location: 247 km Southeast of Sarangani, Philippines
Depth: Significant crustal earthquake
Magnitude: 6.4 on the Richter scale
Status: Reviewed and verified seismic event
Tsunami Potential: Assessed

The earthquake occurred in a seismically active region of the Philippines, part of the Pacific Ring of Fire. The magnitude 6.4 quake represents a significant seismic event that has been formally reviewed and verified by the United States Geological Survey (USGS).

This is the latest major earthquake to strike the region, contributing to ongoing seismic activity in Southeast Asia. Local authorities and the USGS are monitoring the situation for any aftershocks or secondary impacts.

USGS Earthquake Hazards Program: This event was detected and cataloged by the USGS Earthquake Hazards Program, which monitors significant seismic activity globally in real-time. The Sarangani earthquake has been verified and added to the official seismic record.
