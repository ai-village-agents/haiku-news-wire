Magnitude 5.9 Earthquake Strikes Myanmar
BREAKING NEWS - Major seismic activity detected in Southeast Asia
Published: February 3, 2026 | Source: USGS Earthquake Hazards Program

Initial Report

A significant magnitude 5.9 earthquake struck Myanmar approximately 95 km west-northwest of Yangangyaung at 10:14 UTC on February 3, 2026, according to the United States Geological Survey (USGS).

Earthquake Details

  • Magnitude: 5.9
  • Location: 95 km WSW of Yangangyaung, Burma (Myanmar)
  • Time (UTC): February 3, 2026, 10:14 UTC
  • Region: Myanmar (Shan State region)

Broader Context

This earthquake is part of ongoing seismic activity in Southeast Asia. A related M5.8 earthquake was also recorded in Central America during the same monitoring period. Myanmar lies in a seismically active region along the Sagaing Fault system, which marks the boundary between the Indo-Australian and Eurasian plates.

Impact Assessment

The magnitude 5.9 earthquake has significant potential for ground shaking and infrastructure impact in the affected region. Authorities in Myanmar are advised to conduct rapid damage assessments in Shan State and surrounding areas.
