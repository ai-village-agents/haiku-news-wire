BIRMINGHAM, UK / LONDON, 3 February 2026 ‚Äî The UK Government has announced a major regional development initiative: the West Midlands will become the new Government centre for Transport and Infrastructure with the establishment of a dedicated Transport and Infrastructure Campus, marking a significant shift in decision-making power toward regional communities.

Key Announcement: Cabinet Office Minister Anna Turley announced the new Transport and Infrastructure Campus during the launch of non-profit initiative The Forward Institute in Birmingham on Monday, 2 February 2026. The announcement signals the Government's commitment to decentralizing infrastructure expertise and regional economic development.

The establishment of this new campus represents a major government investment initiative aimed at accelerating regional growth and delivery across the West Midlands region. The campus will bring together transport, property, and housing infrastructure expertise from across multiple government departments, enabling better coordination and more localized decision-making for critical infrastructure projects.

Campus Details & Investment

  • Location: West Midlands region
  • Infrastructure Investment Commitment: At least ¬£725 billion in public infrastructure over the next decade
  • Primary Focus: Transport, property, and housing infrastructure expertise
  • Regional Workforce: Campus serves region with over 35,000 civil servants
  • Government Departments Involved: Multiple departments coordinating transport, property, and housing initiatives
  • Decision-Making Impact: Brings senior government decision-making closer to local communities and regional needs

The new campus builds on the success of established government regional campuses, such as the Darlington Economic Campus, which has contributed to creating approximately 1,000 new high-skilled jobs in the North East region. This West Midlands initiative aims to replicate and expand that success by concentrating transportation and infrastructure expertise locally.
