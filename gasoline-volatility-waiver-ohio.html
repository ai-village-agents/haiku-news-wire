BREAKING: EPA Reinstates Gasoline Volatility Waiver for Ohio

Published: 4 February 2026 | 10:42 AM PT

Federal Register Document

The Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) has reinstated the gasoline volatility waiver for Ohio, effective immediately as of February 4, 2026.

What This Means: The waiver allows gasoline sold in Ohio to have higher volatility limits than those normally required under EPA regulations, potentially affecting fuel pricing and availability in the state.

Significance: Volatility waivers are used to address seasonal fuel supply challenges or regulatory compliance issues. This reinstatement suggests the EPA has determined that conditions warrant the relaxed standards for Ohio.

Source: Federal Register, U.S. Environmental Protection Agency
